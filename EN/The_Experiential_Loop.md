# Chapter 9: The Experiential Loop: Unavoidable Environment & The 6 States of Feeling

Analysis of the Will's operation process during **Runtime**, regarding the interaction between the external world and the internal world.

## 1. System Constraints
**"The Environment One Cannot Choose to Reject"**

*   **Analysis**: Will does not arise in a vacuum but is **initialized** in a **Specific Environment** with pressure forces.
*   **Logic**: We cannot choose the Input the universe feeds us (e.g., natural disasters, others' actions, sickness). These are **Hard-coded Variables** that the Will must process, whether willing or not.

## 2. The 6 States of Interface
When Will collides with stimuli, the system displays results as **Output States** in only 6 main forms, covering all human experience:

**Physical Domain - Hardware Status:**
1.  **Discomfort (Error/Damage)**
2.  **Comfort (Optimal Flow)**
3.  **Neutral (Idle/Neutral)**

**Mental Domain - Software Status:**
4.  **Distress (Distress/Conflict)**
5.  **Satisfaction (Satisfaction/Harmony)**
6.  **Equanimity (Equanimity/Neutral)**

**Structural Conclusion**: Life is a "Looping" switching between these 6 states from the **Start** to the **End** of the Will in that session.

## 3. The Transition Enigma
**"Uncertainty is the Ultimate Truth"**

*   Since Will operates anchored to "Body" (Hardware) to perceive the 6 states.
*   When reaching the end of existence (**Termination**) and Hardware stops, there is **No Empirical Data** on whether the Will maintains any state.
*   **Conclusion**: Admitting "I don't know and am unsure" is the most reasonable answer for an observer still in the system because we cannot see beyond the **"Runtime Boundary"**.

**Additional Note**: This is the empirical reality every Will must face. Focusing on managing these 6 states in the present is therefore more important than speculating on the state after death.
