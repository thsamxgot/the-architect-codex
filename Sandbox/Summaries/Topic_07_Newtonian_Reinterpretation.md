*   **Mass ($m$) $\to$ Data Density ($I_{density}$):**

    *   **Concept:** Distance is an illusion rendered for the observer.
    *   **Reality:** In the backend, all data exists in a non-local database. "Position" is just a Memory Address.
*   **Motion ($v$) $\to$ Address Update:**
    *   **Concept:** Movement is not physical travel but the **updating of pointer coordinates** in the system's memory.
*   **Speed of Light ($c$) $\to$ Bandwidth Limit:**
    *   **Concept:** The maximum refresh rate or data transfer speed of the Universal Processor.

## 2. The Three Laws of Motion (System Logic)
**Source:** R02

Newton's laws are not fundamental truths but **Emergent Properties** of resource management.

### Law 1: Inertia (The Law of Caching)
*   **Newton:** Objects keep moving unless acted upon.
*   **Codex:** **System Optimization**.
    *   **Mechanism:** To save processing power, the system **Caches** the current vector of an object (`State_t+1 = State_t + Cache`).
    *   **Logic:** It costs 0 resources to keep rendering the same vector. It costs high resources to *change* the vector (Rewrite).
    *   **Resistance:** Inertia is the system resisting a "Cache Miss" or the cost of rewriting data.

### Law 2: Force ($F=ma$) (The Processing Cost Equation)
*   **Newton:** Force equals Mass times Acceleration.
*   **Codex:** **Processing Cost Equation**.
    *   **Equation:** $\text{Cost} = \text{File Size} \times \text{Update Rate}$
    *   **Mechanism:**
        *   To change the status ($a$) of a large file ($m$), the system demands a massive amount of "Work" ($F$).
        *   Force is literally the **Computing Power** required to overwrite the existing data state.

### Law 3: Action-Reaction (The Feedback Loop)
*   **Newton:** For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
*   **Codex:** **Data Integrity & Zero-Sum Logic**.
    *   **Mechanism:** The universe is a Closed System. You cannot simply "add" momentum to A without "subtracting" it from B.
    *   **Transaction:** The "Reaction" is the system balancing the ledger to ensure Conservation of Data (Energy).

## 3. Gravity: The Great Illusion
**Source:** R02

Newton could describe *how* gravity works, but not *why*. The Codex provides the mechanism.

*   **The flaw in "Attraction":** Objects are NOT pulling each other.
*   **The Reality:** Gravity is **Processing Latency**.
    *   **Mechanism:**
        1.  Massive objects = High Data Density.
        2.  High Density = High Processing Load for the Universal CPU.
        3.  High Load = **Lag** (Time Dilation).
    *   **The Slide:** Objects "slide" towards massive bodies not because of attraction, but because the "Frame Rate" (Time) is slower there. They follow the path of **Least Processing Cost**.
*   **Equation:**
    $$Latency \propto \frac{\text{Data Density}}{\text{Distance}^2}$$

## 4. Origin of Constants
**Source:** R02

Why are $G$ and $c$ constant?
*   **$G$ (Gravitational Constant):** The **Processing Efficiency Coefficient**. How many calculations the system must perform to render 1 unit of Mass.
*   **$c$ (Speed of Light):** The **Hardware Clock Speed**. The absolute limit of how fast the screen can refresh.
